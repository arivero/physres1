# S29 Execution: Covariant Invariant from Angular-Momentum Bivector

## Work Completed
- Defined a covariant scalar using source-velocity projection of orbital angular momentum:
  \[
  \ell:=\frac1m\sqrt{\frac12\,L_{\mu\nu}L^{\mu\nu}},
  \quad
  L^{\mu\nu}=h^\mu{}_\alpha h^\nu{}_\beta J^{\alpha\beta},
  \quad
  h^\mu{}_\nu=\delta^\mu{}_\nu+\frac{U^\mu U_\nu}{c^2}.
  \]
- In the source rest frame, this reduces to
  \[
  \ell=\frac{L}{m}=\gamma r v,
  \qquad
  \dot A_t=\frac{rv}{2}=\frac{\ell}{2\gamma},
  \qquad
  \dot A_\tau=\frac{dA}{d\tau}=\frac{\ell}{2}.
  \]
- For inverse-square gravity (\(K=GMm\)) with SR relation \(v=K/L\), fixing \(\ell\) gives
  \[
  v=\frac{GM}{\ell},
  \qquad
  r(M,\ell)=\frac{\ell^2}{GM}\sqrt{1-\left(\frac{GM}{\ell c}\right)^2},
  \]
  with domain \(\ell>GM/c\).
- Specializing \(\ell=2cL_P\) yields
  \[
  r(M)=\frac{4\hbar}{Mc}\sqrt{1-\frac{M^2}{4M_P^2}},
  \]
  i.e. the D1.5c branch.

## Result
The natural covariant candidate selects the proper-time branch (\(dA/d\tau\) fixed) rather than the coordinate-time branch. So the clock ambiguity can be resolved structurally, but the high-mass asymptotic remains model-level and not yet universal.
