# C02 Debate

## Claim
Euler-Lagrange equations should be presented as a derivation from stationarity with explicit boundary conditions.

## Objection
Omitting function-space assumptions can hide nontrivial analytic requirements.

## Resolution
State assumptions explicitly: \(q\in C^2\) on \([t_i,t_f]\), admissible variations \(\eta\in C_c^\infty((t_i,t_f))\) or fixed-endpoint \(C^1\) variations.

## Status
Accepted.

## Claim
The derivation should explicitly name the fundamental lemma step (why \(\int F\cdot\eta=0\) implies \(F=0\)).

## Objection
This is a standard result and may feel like pedantry.

## Resolution
State it once as a proposition with conditions; it prevents hidden logic jumps and clarifies what changes in `C03` (weak/distributional settings).

## Status
Accepted.
