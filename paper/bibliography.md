# Bibliography Notes

Use this file as a source ledger.

## Citation Key Format
`[AuthorYearShortTag]`

## Source Policy
1. Do not cite `conv_patched.md` or any chat/conversation transcript.
2. Cite external primary sources only (ArXiv, journals, books, archival projects).
3. Treat preprints (including A. Rivero drafts) as guides rather than “source of truth”: use them to locate/structure ideas, but seek independent primary/peer-reviewed support for key claims and label preprint-only content as heuristic until verified.
4. When A. Rivero papers are used for attribution or as a research guide, cite ArXiv identifiers as anchors and avoid presenting them as independent validation.

## Sources
1. `[Newton1687]` Isaac Newton, *Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica* (1687), Book I (limit methods and central-force geometry). Reference access point: Newton Project and standard translated editions.
2. `[Noether1918]` Emmy Noether, "Invariante Variationsprobleme" (1918). English translation commonly used in modern mechanics references.
3. `[Dirac1933]` P. A. M. Dirac, "The Lagrangian in Quantum Mechanics," *Physikalische Zeitschrift der Sowjetunion* 3 (1933), 64–72. (Commonly cited as the earliest explicit path-integral style phase-weighting statement.)

7. `[ConnesKreimer2000]` Alain Connes and Dirk Kreimer, "Renormalization in quantum field theory and the Riemann-Hilbert problem I," *Communications in Mathematical Physics* 210 (2000), 249–273. DOI `10.1007/s002200050779`.

18. `[deGosson2018ShortTimePropagators]` Maurice A. de Gosson, "Short-Time Propagators and the Born–Jordan Quantization Rule," *Entropy* 20(11) (2018), 869. DOI `10.3390/e20110869`. OA: PubMed Central (PMCID: PMC 7512447).

(Contains the unperturbed SR relativistic Kepler equation and discusses circular solutions/constraints.)

26. `[Wilson1974Confinement]` Kenneth G. Wilson, "Confinement of quarks," *Physical Review D* 10 (1974), 2445. DOI `10.1103/PhysRevD.10.2445`. (Original Wilson-loop confinement/area-law context; OA status uncertain.)

28. `[Moyal1949StatisticalQM]` J. E. Moyal, "Quantum mechanics as a statistical theory," *Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society* 45 (1949), 99–124. DOI `10.1017/S0305004100000487`.
