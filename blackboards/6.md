# Blackboard 6 — Parametrized Fresnel Regularization: Composition Test
Date: 2026-02-14
Cycle: S292 (parent: D37). Supersedes S291.

## 1. Regulated kernel (d=1, free particle)

Bare kernel:
\[
K_t(x,y) = \sqrt{\frac{m}{2\pi i\hbar t}}\;\exp\!\left(\frac{im(x-y)^2}{2\hbar t}\right).
\]

Regulated kernel with damping function \(f:[0,\infty)\to[0,\infty)\):
\[
K_t^\varepsilon(x,y) = \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\;\exp\!\left(\frac{im(x-y)^2}{2\hbar t} - \varepsilon\,f\!\left(\frac{(x-y)^2}{t}\right)\right), \quad \varepsilon>0.
\]

## 2. Composition integral

\[
(K_t^\varepsilon \circ K_s^\varepsilon)(x,z) = \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\,\mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(s)
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp\!\left(\frac{im}{2\hbar}\,Q(y) - \varepsilon\left[f\!\left(\frac{(x-y)^2}{t}\right) + f\!\left(\frac{(y-z)^2}{s}\right)\right]\right) dy,
\]
where \(Q(y) = (x-y)^2/t + (y-z)^2/s\).

Completing the square in \(y\) for \(Q(y)\) (from S291):
\[
Q(y) = \frac{t+s}{ts}(y-y_0)^2 + \frac{(x-z)^2}{t+s}, \quad y_0 = \frac{sx+tz}{t+s}.
\]

Set \(u = y - y_0\), \(\Delta = x - z\). Then:
\[
x - y = \frac{s\Delta}{t+s} - u, \qquad y - z = \frac{t\Delta}{t+s} + u.
\]

## 3. Case f(x) = x (standard iε)

Regulator sum:
\[
\varepsilon\left[\frac{(x-y)^2}{t} + \frac{(y-z)^2}{s}\right] = \varepsilon\,Q(y).
\]

**Key structural fact:** \(f(x)=x\) makes the regulator sum proportional to the SAME quadratic form \(Q(y)\) as the oscillatory phase.

Total exponent:
\[
\left(\frac{im}{2\hbar} - \varepsilon\right) Q(y)
= \left(\frac{im}{2\hbar} - \varepsilon\right)\left[\frac{t+s}{ts}\,u^2 + \frac{\Delta^2}{t+s}\right].
\]

### 3a. Gaussian integral

Define \(\alpha_\varepsilon = im/(2\hbar) - \varepsilon\). The \(u\)-integral:
\[
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp\!\left(\alpha_\varepsilon\,\frac{t+s}{ts}\,u^2\right) du.
\]

Since \(\operatorname{Re}(\alpha_\varepsilon) = -\varepsilon < 0\), the integral converges absolutely:
\[
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp\!\left(\alpha_\varepsilon\,\frac{t+s}{ts}\,u^2\right) du
= \sqrt{\frac{\pi ts}{(t+s)(-\alpha_\varepsilon)}}
= \sqrt{\frac{\pi ts}{(t+s)(\varepsilon - im/(2\hbar))}}.
\]

### 3b. Remainder

The non-\(u\) exponent:
\[
\alpha_\varepsilon\,\frac{\Delta^2}{t+s}
= \frac{im\Delta^2}{2\hbar(t+s)} - \varepsilon\,\frac{\Delta^2}{t+s}.
\]

This is precisely the exponent of \(K_{t+s}^\varepsilon(x,z)\) with the **same** \(\varepsilon\) and **same** \(f(x)=x\).

### 3c. Normalization

Full result:
\[
(K_t^\varepsilon \circ K_s^\varepsilon)(x,z)
= \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\,\mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(s)\,
\sqrt{\frac{\pi ts}{(t+s)(\varepsilon - im/(2\hbar))}}\;
\exp\!\left(\frac{im\Delta^2}{2\hbar(t+s)} - \varepsilon\frac{\Delta^2}{t+s}\right).
\]

Setting \(\mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t) = \sqrt{(\varepsilon - im/(2\hbar))/(\pi t)}\) (natural regulated normalization):
\[
\mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\,\mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(s)\,\sqrt{\frac{\pi ts}{(t+s)(\varepsilon-im/(2\hbar))}}
= \frac{\varepsilon - im/(2\hbar)}{\pi\sqrt{ts}}\,\sqrt{\frac{\pi ts}{(t+s)(\varepsilon-im/(2\hbar))}}
= \sqrt{\frac{\varepsilon - im/(2\hbar)}{\pi(t+s)}} = \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t+s).
\]

### 3d. Semigroup closure (f=x)

\[
\boxed{K_t^\varepsilon \circ K_s^\varepsilon = K_{t+s}^\varepsilon \quad \text{(same } \varepsilon \text{, same } f(x)=x \text{).}}
\]

Composition closes **exactly**, with \(\varepsilon\) unchanged (additive semigroup in time, no \(\varepsilon\)-renormalization).

**Equivalence to \(t \to t - i\varepsilon'\):** Setting \(\varepsilon = m\varepsilon'/(2\hbar)\), \(\alpha_\varepsilon = im/(2\hbar(t-i\varepsilon'))\cdot t\). Standard Wick rotation.

## 4. Case f(x) = x² (super-Gaussian regulator)

Regulator sum:
\[
\varepsilon\left[\left(\frac{(x-y)^2}{t}\right)^{\!2} + \left(\frac{(y-z)^2}{s}\right)^{\!2}\right].
\]

In shifted variable \(u = y - y_0\):
\[
\frac{(x-y)^2}{t} = \frac{1}{t}\left(\frac{s\Delta}{t+s} - u\right)^{\!2}, \qquad
\frac{(y-z)^2}{s} = \frac{1}{s}\left(\frac{t\Delta}{t+s} + u\right)^{\!2}.
\]

Each squared term is a polynomial of degree 2 in \(u\). The regulator terms \(((x-y)^2/t)^2\) and \(((y-z)^2/s)^2\) are therefore **degree 4** in \(u\).

### 4a. Integrand structure

Total exponent (quadratic part from phase + quartic part from regulator):
\[
\alpha_0\,\frac{t+s}{ts}\,u^2
- \varepsilon\left[\frac{1}{t^2}\left(\frac{s\Delta}{t+s}-u\right)^{\!4}
+ \frac{1}{s^2}\left(\frac{t\Delta}{t+s}+u\right)^{\!4}\right]
+ (\text{terms independent of } u).
\]

where \(\alpha_0 = im/(2\hbar)\).

### 4b. Expansion of quartic terms

\[
\left(\frac{s\Delta}{t+s}-u\right)^{\!4} = u^4 - 4\frac{s\Delta}{t+s}u^3 + 6\left(\frac{s\Delta}{t+s}\right)^{\!2}u^2 - \cdots
\]

The \(u\)-integral is:
\[
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp\!\left(\alpha_0\frac{t+s}{ts}u^2 - \varepsilon\,P_4(u;\Delta,t,s)\right) du,
\]
where \(P_4\) is a degree-4 polynomial in \(u\) (with \(\Delta\)-dependent coefficients).

### 4c. Non-Gaussian integral — composition failure

This integral is NOT Gaussian. The result has the form (by steepest descent / perturbative expansion in \(\varepsilon\)):
\[
\sim \sqrt{\frac{\pi ts}{i\alpha_0(t+s)}}\;\exp\!\left(\alpha_0\frac{\Delta^2}{t+s}\right)
\left[1 - \varepsilon\,G_2(\Delta,t,s) + O(\varepsilon^2)\right],
\]
where \(G_2\) is a polynomial in \(\Delta\) of degree \(\geq 4\) (from the quartic regulator terms evaluated at the stationary point plus fluctuation corrections).

**The \(O(\varepsilon)\) correction is NOT of the form \(\exp(-\varepsilon(\Delta^2/(t+s))^2)\).**

Explicitly, the leading \(\varepsilon\) correction from evaluating the quartic regulator at \(u=0\):
\[
\varepsilon\left[\frac{s^4\Delta^4}{t^2(t+s)^4} + \frac{t^4\Delta^4}{s^2(t+s)^4}\right]
= \frac{\varepsilon\Delta^4(s^6+t^6)}{t^2 s^2(t+s)^4}.
\]

For the target form \(K_{t+s}^\varepsilon\) with \(f(x)=x^2\), the regulator would be:
\[
\varepsilon\left(\frac{\Delta^2}{t+s}\right)^{\!2} = \frac{\varepsilon\Delta^4}{(t+s)^2}.
\]

These are NOT equal:
\[
\frac{s^6+t^6}{t^2 s^2(t+s)^2} \neq 1 \quad\text{for } t \neq s.
\]

(Check: for \(t=s\), \((s^6+t^6)/(t^2s^2(t+s)^2) = 2s^6/(s^2 s^2 4s^2) = 2/4 = 1/2 \neq 1\).)

### 4d. Verdict (f=x²)

\[
\boxed{K_t^\varepsilon \circ K_s^\varepsilon \neq K_{t+s}^{\varepsilon'} \quad\text{for any } \varepsilon'(t,s,\varepsilon) \text{ when } f(x)=x^2.}
\]

The quartic regulator breaks semigroup closure at \(O(\varepsilon)\). The composition produces a result whose \(\Delta\)-dependence in the regulator involves \(t,s\) in a form that cannot be absorbed into a single-parameter kernel \(K_{t+s}^{\varepsilon'}\).

## 5. General f: Why linearity is forced

### 5a. Structural argument

For the composition integral, completing the square in \(y\) gives:
\[
\text{oscillatory: } \frac{im}{2\hbar}\,Q(y), \qquad
\text{regulator: } -\varepsilon\left[f\!\left(\frac{(x-y)^2}{t}\right) + f\!\left(\frac{(y-z)^2}{s}\right)\right].
\]

The oscillatory part is quadratic in \(y\). The Gaussian integral closes the semigroup if and only if the total exponent is quadratic in \(y\).

**Condition for semigroup closure:** The regulator sum \(f((x-y)^2/t) + f((y-z)^2/s)\) must be a quadratic polynomial in \(y\) (plus terms independent of \(y\)).

Define \(a = (x-y)^2/t\) and \(b = (y-z)^2/s\). Note \(a\) and \(b\) are each quadratic in \(y\). Then:
- \(f(a) + f(b)\) is quadratic in \(y\) iff \(f\) is linear (degree 1 polynomial).

**Proof.** Write \(f(x) = c_1 x + c_2 x^2 + c_3 x^3 + \cdots\). Then:
- \(f(a)\) has degree \(2\deg(f)\) in \(y\) (since \(a\) is quadratic in \(y\)).
- For the sum to be degree 2 in \(y\), need \(\deg(f) \leq 1\).
- \(f(x) = c_0 + c_1 x\): the constant \(c_0\) contributes an overall factor \(e^{-2\varepsilon c_0}\) (absorbed in normalization).
- Effective constraint: \(f(x) = c_1 x\) with \(c_1 > 0\) (monotone damping).

By rescaling \(\varepsilon \to c_1\varepsilon\), WLOG \(f(x) = x\). \(\square\)

### 5b. Polynomial regulators: systematic exclusion

| \(f(x)\) | Degree in \(y\) | Gaussian? | Semigroup? |
|---|---|---|---|
| \(x\) (linear) | 2 | YES | YES (Section 3) |
| \(x^2\) (quadratic) | 4 | NO | NO (Section 4) |
| \(x^n\), \(n \geq 2\) | \(2n\) | NO | NO |
| \(\sqrt{x}\) | 1 | YES (linear in \(y\)) | — see 5c |
| \(\log(1+x)\) | transcendental | NO | NO |

### 5c. The f(x) = sqrt(x) case

\(f((x-y)^2/t) = |x-y|/\sqrt{t}\): linear in \(|y|\), giving \(\int\exp(i\alpha u^2 - \beta|u+a| - \gamma|u-b|)\,du\). Evaluates via error functions, not Gaussian. Result not of form \(\exp(-\varepsilon'|\Delta|/\sqrt{t+s})\). Semigroup fails.

## 6. Identity limit

### 6a. f(x) = x

As \(t \to 0^+\):
\[
K_t^\varepsilon(x,y) = \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\,\exp\!\left(\left(\frac{im}{2\hbar}-\varepsilon\right)\frac{(x-y)^2}{t}\right).
\]

For \(x \neq y\): \((x-y)^2/t \to +\infty\), and \(\operatorname{Re}(\text{coeff}) = -\varepsilon < 0\), so the exponential vanishes.

At \(x = y\): exponent = 0, kernel = \(\mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t) \to \infty\).

Pairing with test function \(\varphi\):
\[
\langle K_t^\varepsilon, \varphi\rangle = \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\int \exp\!\left(\alpha_\varepsilon\frac{u^2}{t}\right)\varphi(y+u)\,du.
\]

Rescale \(v = u/\sqrt{t}\):
\[
= \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\sqrt{t}\int \exp(\alpha_\varepsilon v^2)\,\varphi(y+v\sqrt{t})\,dv
\to \varphi(y) \quad\text{as } t \to 0^+.
\]

The \(\varepsilon \to 0^+\) limit recovers the Fresnel nascent delta.

**Identity limit: satisfied for all \(\varepsilon > 0\), with clean \(\varepsilon \to 0^+\) limit.**

### 6b. f(x) = x²

\[
K_t^\varepsilon(x,y) \sim \exp\!\left(\frac{im(x-y)^2}{2\hbar t} - \varepsilon\frac{(x-y)^4}{t^2}\right).
\]

As \(t \to 0^+\) with \(u = x-y\) fixed, \(u \neq 0\):
- Oscillatory part: \(e^{imu^2/(2\hbar t)}\) oscillates
- Damping: \(e^{-\varepsilon u^4/t^2} \to 0\) (quartic damping)

The quartic damping does suppress the kernel for \(x \neq y\), giving nascent-delta behavior. However, this is **moot** because f=x² already fails composition (Section 4).

## 7. Theorem (Main result)

**Theorem.** Among all regulators of the form
\[
K_t^\varepsilon(x,y) = \mathcal{N}_\varepsilon(t)\,\exp\!\left(\frac{im(x-y)^2}{2\hbar t} - \varepsilon\,f\!\left(\frac{(x-y)^2}{t}\right)\right)
\]
with \(f:[0,\infty)\to[0,\infty)\) continuous and monotone nondecreasing, the following are equivalent:
1. Composition semigroup: \(K_t^\varepsilon \circ K_s^\varepsilon = K_{t+s}^\varepsilon\) for all \(t,s>0\)
2. \(f(x) = cx\) for some \(c > 0\) (linear)

Rescaling \(\varepsilon \to c\varepsilon\), the unique regulator (up to trivial rescaling) is:
\[
\boxed{f(x) = x \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \text{i}\varepsilon\text{ prescription } (t \to t - i\varepsilon').}
\]

**Proof sketch.** (\(\Leftarrow\)) Section 3. (\(\Rightarrow\)) Section 5a: semigroup closure requires the total exponent to be quadratic in the integration variable \(y\). Since \((x-y)^2/t\) is quadratic in \(y\), \(f(a)\) is degree \(2\deg(f)\) in \(y\). Degree \(\leq 2\) forces \(\deg(f) \leq 1\). Constant part absorbed in normalization. \(\square\)

**Corollary.** The iε prescription is not external input for P4.2: it is the unique regulator compatible with the composition semigroup (C).

## 8. Connection to S288 (Blackboard 1)

S288 showed: composition (C) + locality (L\_loc) forces exponential weight \(W = e^{c_0 S/\kappa}\).

S292 shows: composition (C) + parametric regularization forces \(f(x) = x\) (linear damping = iε).

**Parallel structure:** Both results derive a "standard" prescription from the semigroup law. The exponential weight and the iε prescription are both **theorems of composition**, not independent assumptions.

## 9. Scope and caveats

1. **1D free particle only** — but the argument (degree-counting in \(y\)) is dimension-independent.
2. **Polynomial regulators** — the degree argument extends to all smooth \(f\) via Taylor expansion at any point. Non-smooth \(f\) (e.g., \(\sqrt{x}\)) fail for different reasons (Section 5c).
3. **Normalization ambiguity:** We assumed regulators of the specific form \(\exp(-\varepsilon f(\cdot))\). More general damping (e.g., multiplicative cutoffs) would require separate analysis.
4. **The argument does NOT use the identity limit (I).** Composition alone forces \(f = x\). The identity limit is then automatically satisfied (Section 6a).
