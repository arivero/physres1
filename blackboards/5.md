## 1. Setup: short-time kernel on (M, g)

Let (M, g) be a d-dimensional Riemannian manifold (geodesically complete, no
conjugate points in the time interval considered). Synge's world function:
\[
\sigma(x,y) = \tfrac{1}{2}d_g(x,y)^2.
\]

Van Vleck-Morette determinant:
\[
\Delta(x,y) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{|g(x)||g(y)|}}\det\!\bigl(-\partial^2\sigma/\partial x^i\partial y^j\bigr).
\]

Short-time propagator (DeWitt ansatz):
\[
K(x,y;t) = \bigl(2\pi\kappa t\bigr)^{-d/2}\,\Delta^{1/2}(x,y)\,
\exp\!\bigl(iS_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,y;t)/\kappa\bigr)\,\bigl[1+O(t)\bigr],
\]
where \(S_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,y;t) = m\sigma(x,y)/(2t)\) for free geodesic motion.

**Half-density kernel.** Define the bi-half-density kernel:
\[
\widetilde{K}(x,y;t) = |g(x)|^{1/4}\,K(x,y;t)\,|g(y)|^{1/4}.
\]

## 2. Composition integral on curved M

The semigroup law on (M, g) reads:
\[
K(x,z;t_1+t_2) = \int_M K(x,y;t_1)\,K(y,z;t_2)\,\sqrt{|g(y)|}\,d^d y.
\]

Substituting the short-time form:
\[
K(x,z;T) = \bigl(2\pi\kappa\bigr)^{-d}\,(t_1 t_2)^{-d/2}
\int_M \Delta^{1/2}(x,y)\,\Delta^{1/2}(y,z)\,
\exp\!\Bigl(\frac{i}{\kappa}\bigl[S_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,y;t_1)+S_{\mathrm{cl}}(y,z;t_2)\bigr]\Bigr)
\sqrt{|g(y)|}\,d^d y \;\bigl[1+O(t)\bigr],
\]
where \(T = t_1+t_2\).

## 3. Evaluation in geodesic normal coordinates at stationary point

### 3a. Stationary phase point

The phase is \(\Phi(y) = S_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,y;t_1) + S_{\mathrm{cl}}(y,z;t_2)\).
For free geodesic motion: \(S_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,y;t) = m\sigma(x,y)/(2t)\).

Stationary condition \(\partial\Phi/\partial y^i = 0\):
\[
\frac{m}{2t_1}\frac{\partial\sigma(x,y)}{\partial y^i}
+ \frac{m}{2t_2}\frac{\partial\sigma(y,z)}{\partial y^i} = 0.
\]

Using \(\nabla_y\sigma(x,y) = -\exp_y^{-1}(x)\), the stationary condition is:
\[
-\frac{m}{2t_1}\exp_y^{-1}(x) - \frac{m}{2t_2}\exp_y^{-1}(z) = 0,
\]
which gives: y lies on the geodesic from x to z at fractional parameter
\(\lambda = t_1/(t_1+t_2)\). Call this \(y_* = \gamma_{xz}(\lambda)\).

### 3b. Normal coordinates centered at \(y_*\)

In Riemannian normal coordinates \(\{\xi^i\}\) centered at \(y_*\):
\[
g_{ij}(\xi) = \delta_{ij} - \tfrac{1}{3}R_{ikjl}\xi^k\xi^l + O(\xi^3),
\]
\[
\sqrt{|g(\xi)|} = 1 - \tfrac{1}{6}R_{kl}\xi^k\xi^l + O(\xi^3).
\]

### 3c. Hessian of the phase

\[
H_{ij} = \frac{\partial^2\Phi}{\partial\xi^i\partial\xi^j}\bigg|_{\xi=0}
= \frac{mT}{t_1 t_2}\Bigl[\delta_{ij}
+ \frac{t_1 t_2}{3T}\,R_{ikjl}\,v^k v^l + O(R^2)\Bigr],
\]
where \(v^i\) is the geodesic tangent at \(y_*\). Leading order: \(H_{ij}=(mT/(t_1 t_2))\delta_{ij}\).

### 3d. Gaussian integration

Stationary phase integration:
\[
\int d^d\xi\;\exp\!\Bigl(\frac{i}{2\kappa}\xi^i H_{ij}\xi^j\Bigr)\,\sqrt{|g(\xi)|}
= \bigl(2\pi\kappa\bigr)^{d/2}\,(\det H)^{-1/2}\,\bigl[1+O(R)\bigr].
\]

At leading order:
\[
(\det H)^{-1/2} = \Bigl(\frac{mT}{t_1 t_2}\Bigr)^{-d/2}
\bigl[1 + O(R)\bigr].
\]

## 4. Van Vleck factorization

### 4a. Statement

**Claim.** At leading order in curvature:
\[
\Delta^{1/2}(x,y_*)\,\Delta^{1/2}(y_*,z)\,\sqrt{|g(y_*)|}\,(\det H)^{-1/2}
= \Delta^{1/2}(x,z)\,\bigl[1+O(R)\bigr].
\]

### 4b. Verification (flat limit)

On \(\mathbb{R}^d\): \(\Delta=1\), \(|g|=1\), \(H = (mT/(t_1 t_2))\mathbf{1}\).
Gaussian factor: \((2\pi\kappa)^{d/2}(mT/(t_1 t_2))^{-d/2}\).
\[
\Bigl(\frac{m}{2\pi\kappa t_1}\Bigr)^{d/2}\Bigl(\frac{m}{2\pi\kappa t_2}\Bigr)^{d/2}
\cdot\Bigl(\frac{2\pi\kappa t_1 t_2}{mT}\Bigr)^{d/2}
= \Bigl(\frac{m}{2\pi\kappa T}\Bigr)^{d/2}. \quad\checkmark
\]

### 4c. Curved-space Van Vleck factorization

The Van Vleck determinant satisfies the **transport equation** (DeWitt 1965,
Christensen 1976):
\[
\nabla_y^\mu\bigl(\Delta(x,y)\,\nabla_{y\mu}\sigma(x,y)\bigr) = d\,\Delta(x,y).
\]

**Composition law for Van Vleck determinants** (Visser 1993, DeWitt-Morette 1976):
\[
\boxed{
\Delta(x,z) = \frac{\det(-\partial^2 S_{\mathrm{cl,1}}/\partial x^i\partial y^j)\,
\det(-\partial^2 S_{\mathrm{cl,2}}/\partial y^i\partial z^j)}
{\det(-\partial^2(S_{\mathrm{cl,1}}+S_{\mathrm{cl,2}})/\partial y^i\partial y^j)}\,
\frac{1}{|g(y_*)|^{1/2}}
}
\]
evaluated at the stationary point \(y_*\).

Rewriting in terms of Van Vleck determinants:
\[
\Delta(x,z)\,\sqrt{|g(x)||g(z)|} = \frac{\Delta(x,y_*)\sqrt{|g(x)||g(y_*)|}\;\cdot\;
\Delta(y_*,z)\sqrt{|g(y_*)||g(z)|}}
{|g(y_*)|\;\det H / \det_{\mathrm{flat}}H}.
\]

**Upshot:** The Van Vleck determinants compose correctly through stationary phase.
The factor \(\sqrt{|g(y_*)|}\) from the Riemannian volume measure is absorbed into
the composition formula. No extra \(\kappa\)-dependent factors appear.

## 5. Dimensional analysis: what carries [action]?

### 5a. Dimension count on curved M

| Quantity | Dimensions |
|---|---|
| \(S_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,y;t)\) | \([\mathrm{action}] = ML^2T^{-1}\) |
| \(\kappa\) | \([\mathrm{action}]\) |
| \(\sigma(x,y)\) | \([L^2]\) |
| \(\Delta(x,y)\) | **dimensionless** (ratio of determinants) |
| \(\sqrt{|g(y)|}\) | coordinate density, \([L^d]\) in coordinate units |
| \(R_{ijkl}\) | \([L^{-2}]\) |
| \(t\) | \([T]\) |
| \(m\) | \([M]\) |

### 5b. Key observation

\(\Delta(x,y)\) is constructed entirely from the metric \(g\) and coordinates.
It involves **no action-dimensional quantities**. Explicitly:
\[
\Delta(x,y) = \frac{\det(-\partial_x^i\partial_y^j\sigma)}{\sqrt{|g(x)||g(y)|}},
\]
where \(\sigma\) has dimension \([L^2]\) and the derivatives \(\partial_x\partial_y\sigma\)
are dimensionless (length²/length² in normal coordinates).

**The Van Vleck determinant is purely geometric (metric-dependent, \(\kappa\)-independent).**

### 5c. Where \(\kappa\) enters

In the composition integral, \(\kappa\) enters **only** through:
1. The phase: \(\exp(iS_{\mathrm{cl}}/\kappa)\)
2. The normalization prefactor: \((2\pi\kappa t)^{-d/2}\)
3. The stationary-phase Gaussian width: \(\propto \kappa/H\)

The Gaussian integration produces a factor \((2\pi\kappa)^{d/2}/(\det H)^{1/2}\).
Since \(\det H \propto m^d\) (geometric, no \(\kappa\)), the \(\kappa\)-counting is:
\[
(2\pi\kappa)^{-d}\cdot(2\pi\kappa)^{d/2} = (2\pi\kappa)^{-d/2}.
\]

This reproduces the target normalization \((2\pi\kappa T)^{-d/2}\) exactly as on
\(\mathbb{R}^d\). **Curvature does not alter the \(\kappa\)-power-counting.**

## 6. P4.2 hypotheses on curved M

### (C) Composition
\[
K(x,z;T) = \int_M K(x,y;t_1)\,K(y,z;t_2)\,\mathrm{dvol}_g(y).
\]
The volume element \(\mathrm{dvol}_g = \sqrt{|g|}\,d^dy\) is the natural
Riemannian measure. This is coordinate-invariant.

### (L) Local exponential weight
\[
K_{\Delta t}(x,y) = \mathcal{N}(\Delta t)\,\Delta^{1/2}(x,y)\,
\exp\!\bigl(c_0\,S_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,y;\Delta t)\bigr).
\]
The prefactor \(\Delta^{1/2}\) is the curved-space replacement for the trivial
"1" in flat space. It is metric-dependent but \(\kappa\)-independent.

### (I) Identity limit
As \(t\to 0^+\), the kernel must approach the Riemannian delta:
\[
K(x,y;t) \to \delta_g(x,y) = |g(x)|^{-1/2}\delta^{(d)}(x-y).
\]
In half-density language: \(\widetilde{K}\to\delta^{(d)}(x-y)\), the coordinate delta.
The Van Vleck factor \(\Delta^{1/2}(x,y)\to 1\) as \(y\to x\), so the identity
limit is controlled entirely by the normalization \(\mathcal{N}(t)\) and the
phase \(\exp(i\sigma/(2\kappa t))\), exactly as on \(\mathbb{R}^d\).

### (D) Dimensional homogeneity
\([K] = [\text{length}]^{-d}\) (scalar kernel) or \([K] = 1\) (half-density kernel).
Unchanged from flat space.

## 7. Verdict

**P4.2 extends cleanly to curved configuration spaces.**

The argument proceeds in three steps:

**(i)** The composition integral on (M, g) introduces the Van Vleck determinant
\(\Delta^{1/2}\) and the volume element \(\sqrt{|g|}\). Both are purely geometric:
they depend on the metric but carry no \(\kappa\)-dependence.

**(ii)** Stationary-phase evaluation reproduces the flat-space \(\kappa\)-counting
exactly:
\[
\text{two kernels: } (2\pi\kappa)^{-d} \quad\times\quad
\text{Gaussian: } (2\pi\kappa)^{+d/2} \quad=\quad (2\pi\kappa)^{-d/2}.
\]
The \(d/2\) exponent is forced by the same semigroup functional equation
\(\mathcal{N}(T) = \mathcal{N}(t_1)\mathcal{N}(t_2)\cdot f(t_1,t_2)\) as on
\(\mathbb{R}^d\); curvature enters only in subleading corrections (\(O(R)\) in
the Seeley-DeWitt coefficients \(a_n\)).

**(iii)** The necessity of \(\kappa\neq 0,\infty\) is unchanged: the identity
limit requires non-degenerate Gaussian spreading (same \(\kappa\to 0\) obstruction),
and dynamical content requires non-trivial phase weighting (\(\kappa\to\infty\)
obstruction). The metric provides no escape route because \(\Delta\) and \(\sqrt{|g|}\)
are dimensionless or carry only geometric dimensions — they cannot substitute for
the action-dimensional scale \(\kappa\).

**Summary formula.** On (M, g):
\[
\boxed{
K(x,z;T) = \Bigl(\frac{m}{2\pi\kappa T}\Bigr)^{d/2}\Delta^{1/2}(x,z)\,
\exp\!\Bigl(\frac{i\,S_{\mathrm{cl}}(x,z;T)}{\kappa}\Bigr)
\bigl[1 + O(R\cdot t)\bigr].
}
\]

The action-dimensional constant \(\kappa\) is structurally necessary on any
Riemannian manifold, not just \(\mathbb{R}^d\). The Van Vleck determinant,
despite being metric-dependent, is \(\kappa\)-independent and composes correctly
through the semigroup law.

## 8. Connection to P4.2 hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Flat \(\mathbb{R}^d\) | Curved (M,g) | Change? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(C)** Composition | \(\int dq\) | \(\int\sqrt{|g|}\,d^dy\) | Measure only |
| **(L)** Local exp | \(e^{c_0 S}\) | \(\Delta^{1/2}e^{c_0 S}\) | Geometric prefactor |
| **(I)** Identity | \(\delta^{(d)}\) | \(\delta_g\) | Same in half-density |
| **(D)** Dimensions | \([K]=[L^{-d}]\) | \([K]=[L^{-d}]\) | Unchanged |

No hypothesis weakened. The curved-space extension requires only:
- **(L')**: \(K = \mathcal{N}(t)\,\Delta^{1/2}(x,y)\exp(c_0 S_{\mathrm{cl}})\), where
  \(\Delta^{1/2}\) is the Van Vleck half-density (geometric, \(\kappa\)-independent).

This is a **natural enrichment**, not a modification, of the flat-space hypothesis.
