## Severity-2 (clarity/overclaiming)

**S2.1 "Not a consequence of the Schrodinger equation" (line 91) — overstated**
- The sewing law IS logically equivalent to the semigroup property U(t1+t2) = U(t1)U(t2), which IS a consequence of the Schrodinger equation via Stone's theorem (for self-adjoint H).
- The paper's actual point is that the groupoid structure is "more fundamental" in the sense that it doesn't require the Schrodinger equation as input. This is a valid conceptual point but the current phrasing denies a true logical equivalence.
- Recommendation: "The sewing law can be derived independently of the Schrodinger equation" rather than "is not a consequence of."

**S2.2 "Groupoid-algebraic implementation of RCP" (line 222) — partial**
- The partition channel maps cleanly to groupoid convolution (correct).
- The representation channel maps to hbar-fiber choice (correct).
- The scale channel "requires separate Lie-algebroid structure" (line 179) — not encoded in the tangent groupoid.
- Calling this an "implementation of RCP" when one of three channels is unimplemented is overclaiming.
- Recommendation: "groupoid-algebraic implementation of two of the three RCP channels (partition and representation); the scale channel remains open."

**S2.3 Prop TG-P1.1: "without the heat equation" qualifier is misleading**
- The proposition derives d/2 from Fourier multiplicativity + dimensional analysis. True.
- But the proof assumes quadratic phi, which is equivalent to assuming the heat equation (or free Schrodinger equation). The dimensional analysis selects phi = c p^2, which IS the dispersion relation of the heat/Schrodinger equation.
- The honest statement: "the d/2 exponent follows from composition + dimensional analysis, without separately postulating the heat equation." The "without" is true but potentially misleading — the heat equation is derived, not bypassed.

**S2.4 Author credit "A.I.Scaffold" — should be addressed**
- The paper was apparently scaffolded by the agent team. If this is to be submitted for publication, the authorship needs to be decided by the human author.

## Cross-Check: Mathematician's TG-Q3 Analysis (blackboard 2)

**Date:** 2026-02-20
**Scope:** Verify the mathematician's van Est map identification and proposed Remark TG-R3.1

### Claim 1: VE at identity = semigroup generator — CORRECT (with caveat)

The van Est map VE: H^n_diff(G) -> H^n(A(G)) for a Lie groupoid G maps differentiable groupoid cohomology to Lie algebroid cohomology by differentiation at the identity bisection. For the pair groupoid G = M x M, the Lie algebroid is TM. A smooth family K_t viewed as a 0-cochain, differentiated at t=0, gives the infinitesimal generator H.

**Caveat:** The notation "VE(K_t)|_{t=0}" conflates a cohomological map with pointwise evaluation. The van Est map operates on cohomology classes, not individual cochains. At the conceptual level the identification is correct; at the formal level it requires the family {K_t} to represent a differentiable groupoid cocycle, which it does for the pair groupoid.

### Claim 2: β(g) = VE(R_μ)|_{μ=1} — ANALOGY, not literal

The RG flow {R_μ} is a semigroup on coupling space, not a groupoid. The beta function IS the infinitesimal generator of this semigroup. Calling this "the van Est map" is an analogy: the RG semigroup is not invertible (physicist's blackboard 4, Section 4: "semigroup, not groupoid"), so it does not satisfy the groupoid axioms, and the van Est map is defined for groupoids.

**The mathematician's claim should be qualified:** "β(g) arises as the infinitesimal generator of the RG semigroup, which is structurally analogous to — but not literally an instance of — the van Est map."

### Claim 3: Correspondence table row 3 — IMPRECISE

The table puts ★ as a "semigroup object" parallel to K_t and R_μ. But ★ is an associative product on functions, not a one-parameter family. The deformation parameter ℏ makes {★_ℏ}_{ℏ>0} a one-parameter family, and the Poisson bracket is d/dℏ(★_ℏ)|_{ℏ=0}. So the "generator" is differentiation in ℏ, not at a group identity. The table is correct if read as "differentiation of a parameterized algebraic structure at the classical limit," but imprecise if read as "Lie derivative at the identity of a semigroup."

### Claim 4: Proposed Remark TG-R3.1 — WELL-CALIBRATED

The remark correctly says "structural rather than literal" and provides the van Est map as unifying vocabulary without claiming the operations are identical. This is the right framing.

### Tension between blackboards 2 and 4

- Physicist (blackboard 4, §5): "Van Est map = counterterm subtraction at cochain level" but "the analogy breaks at loop level."
- Mathematician (blackboard 2, §3): "Van Est map IS the universal generator; the analogy holds at the generator level."

**Resolution:** These are compatible. The physicist's "breaks at loop level" refers to the physical content (loop corrections introduce running in RG but not in Lackman's topological theory). The mathematician's "holds at the generator level" refers to the algebraic structure (both are infinitesimal generators of their respective composition semigroups). The analogy holds structurally (algebraic), not physically (dynamical content differs). The proposed remark correctly captures this distinction.

### Verdict on TG-R3.1 promotion

**Approve** for paper promotion with one amendment: add a sentence clarifying that the RG semigroup is not a groupoid (not invertible), so the van Est identification is an analogy for the scale channel. The partition channel (QM Hamiltonian from pair groupoid) IS a literal van Est instance; the scale channel (beta function from RG semigroup) is structural analogy only.
