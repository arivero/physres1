# RG-Fundamental Revision Support

## 1. 3D Loop Integral: Referee-2 M3 Rebuttal

**Ref-2 M3 claim:** Im(I_{3D}) should be $-i(m/4\pi\hbar^2)k$, not $-i(m/2\pi\hbar^2)k$.

**Paper's formula (Section 5.8.1 and 5.9):**
$$I_3(E;\Lambda) = -\frac{m}{\pi^2\hbar^2}\Lambda - i\frac{m}{2\pi\hbar^2}k + O(k^2/\Lambda).$$

### Verification

Convention: $I_d(E;\Lambda) = \int_{|q|<\Lambda} \frac{d^dq}{(2\pi)^d} \frac{1}{E - \hbar^2q^2/(2m) + i0}$.

For d=3, $E = \hbar^2 k^2/(2m)$. Sokhotski-Plemelj:
$$\text{Im}(I_3) = -\pi \int_{|q|<\Lambda} \frac{d^3q}{(2\pi)^3} \delta\!\left(\frac{\hbar^2(k^2-q^2)}{2m}\right).$$

Delta function chain rule:
$$\delta\!\left(\frac{\hbar^2(k^2-q^2)}{2m}\right) = \frac{2m}{\hbar^2} \cdot \frac{1}{2k}\delta(|q|-k) = \frac{m}{\hbar^2 k}\delta(|q|-k).$$

Angular integration: $\int d^3q \to \int_0^\Lambda 4\pi q^2\, dq$.

$$\text{Im}(I_3) = -\pi \cdot \frac{4\pi}{(2\pi)^3} \cdot k^2 \cdot \frac{m}{\hbar^2 k} = -\pi \cdot \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \cdot \frac{mk}{\hbar^2} = -\frac{mk}{2\pi\hbar^2}.$$

**Result:** $\text{Im}(I_3) = -mk/(2\pi\hbar^2)$. **Paper is CORRECT.**

### Referee's error
Ref-2 writes: "$(1/2\pi)^2 \cdot (-i\pi) \cdot (m/\hbar^2) \cdot k = -i(mk)/(4\pi\hbar^2)$".

The factor $(1/2\pi)^2 = 1/(4\pi^2)$ is WRONG for d=3. The correct measure factor is:
$$\frac{4\pi}{(2\pi)^3} = \frac{4\pi}{8\pi^3} = \frac{1}{2\pi^2}.$$

Referee used $(2\pi)^{-2}$ (d=2 convention) instead of $4\pi \cdot (2\pi)^{-3}$ (d=3). This gives $1/(4\pi^2)$ instead of $1/(2\pi^2)$, hence the factor-of-2 discrepancy.

**Recommendation:** Contest M3 in response letter with this explicit calculation.
