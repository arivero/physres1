## 2. Setup: Yang-Baxter and q-Deformed Convolution

**Standard convolution (C):**
K_{t₁+t₂}(x,y) = ∫ K_{t₁}(x,z) K_{t₂}(z,y) d^dz

**q-Deformed analog — candidate (C_q):**
Replace the commutative integral over z with a noncommutative product:
K_{t₁+t₂} = K_{t₁} ∗_q K_{t₂}

where ∗_q is a q-deformed convolution (twist product in a Hopf algebra).

**Precise definition (Drinfeld twist):**
Given a Hopf algebra H with R-matrix R ∈ H⊗H satisfying Yang-Baxter (YBE):
(R⊗1)(1⊗R)(R⊗1) = (1⊗R)(R⊗1)(1⊗R)

The twist product on A = Fun(G) (functions on group G):
(f ∗_q g)(x) = m ∘ R ∘ (f ⊗ g)(x)

where m is the original multiplication and R acts via the coproduct.

## 5. Mathematical Obstruction to Forcing q via (C) Alone

**Claim (new, mathematician):**
The q-deformed sewing axiom (C_q) does NOT force q to be a root of unity.

**Argument:**
(i) For any q ∈ ℂ*, the quantum group U_q(g) has a coproduct Δ_q: U_q(g) → U_q(g)⊗U_q(g).
(ii) The coproduct satisfies (Δ_q⊗1)∘Δ_q = (1⊗Δ_q)∘Δ_q [coassociativity = semigroup law].
(iii) Coassociativity holds for ALL q, not just roots of unity.
(iv) Therefore, (C_q) is satisfied for all q — no forcing.

**What DOES force q = root of unity:**
Roots of unity emerge from REPRESENTATION THEORY constraints:
- U_q(g) at q = e^{iπ/κ} (κ = k + g∨): Verma modules have a finite-dimensional quotient
- The quotient gives a SEMISIMPLE category (Verlinde formula)
- Semisimplicity = unitarity requirement (positive inner product on Hilbert space)

**Conclusion (Q1a negative, sev-2):**
(C_q) [coassociativity] does not force q. Unitarity forces q to roots of unity.
This is the q-deformed analog of the OQ3 negative result:
- OQ3: (C) does not force positivity; unitarity+Lorentz forces it
- CFT-Q1: (C_q) does not force root-of-unity q; unitarity+representation forces it

## 10. Physicist Assessment: WZW and the Physical Mechanism for q-Forcing

**Physical path to q = e^{2πi/(k+g∨)}:**

**P1: Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) consistency.**

Level k must be integer: the Wess-Zumino term is well-defined mod 2π only for k ∈ ℤ (Witten 1983).
This is a PATH INTEGRAL composition constraint — the phase ambiguity of the WZ term is 2πk.
Composition = gluing Σ₁ ∪ Σ₂ forces k ∈ ℤ (otherwise sewing gives inconsistent phases).

**P2: Verlinde formula and modular invariance.**
The torus partition function Z(τ) = Σ_λ |χ_λ(τ)|² must be modular invariant.
χ_λ(τ) = q^{h_λ - c/24} Σ_n d_n q^n, q = e^{2πiτ}.
For the sum to close under SL(2,ℤ): h_λ - c/24 must be rational, forcing k ∈ ℤ.
**This IS a composition constraint**: modular invariance = consistency of torus sewing.

**P3: Surgery exact (RT) formula.**
Z(M) = Σ_λ (S_{0λ}/S_{00})^{-χ(M)/2} · (RT invariant).
For Z(M) to be a topological invariant under Dehn surgery: q must be a root of unity.
Non-root-of-unity q gives ill-defined surgery phases (Kirby calculus inconsistency).

**Physical summary:**
