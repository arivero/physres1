## 1. Mehler kernel (1D Lorentzian, V = (1/2)mω²q²)

\[
K_t(x,y) = \sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{2\pi i\hbar\sin\omega t}}\;
\exp\!\left[\frac{im\omega}{2\hbar\sin\omega t}
\Bigl((x^2+y^2)\cos\omega t - 2xy\Bigr)\right].
\]

Shorthand: define \(\alpha \equiv m\omega/(2\hbar)\), \(c_t \equiv \cos\omega t\), \(s_t \equiv \sin\omega t\). Then:
\[
K_t(x,y) = \sqrt{\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\, s_t}}\;
\exp\!\left[\frac{i\alpha}{s_t}\bigl((x^2+y^2)c_t - 2xy\bigr)\right].
\]

## 2. Composition integral

Target:
\[
(K_t \circ K_s)(x,z) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} K_t(x,y)\,K_s(y,z)\,dy.
\]

### 2a. Prefactor

\[
\text{Prefactor} = \sqrt{\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\,s_t}} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\,s_s}}
= \frac{\alpha}{\pi i\sqrt{s_t\,s_s}}.
\]

### 2b. Exponent (action sum)

\[
E(y) \equiv \frac{i\alpha}{s_t}\bigl[(x^2+y^2)c_t - 2xy\bigr]
+ \frac{i\alpha}{s_s}\bigl[(y^2+z^2)c_s - 2yz\bigr].
\]

Collect terms in \(y\):
\[
E(y) = i\alpha\left(\frac{c_t}{s_t}+\frac{c_s}{s_s}\right)y^2
- 2i\alpha\left(\frac{x}{s_t}+\frac{z}{s_s}\right)y
+ i\alpha\left(\frac{x^2 c_t}{s_t}+\frac{z^2 c_s}{s_s}\right).
\]

### 2c. Quadratic coefficient

\[
A \equiv \frac{c_t}{s_t}+\frac{c_s}{s_s}
= \frac{c_t s_s + c_s s_t}{s_t s_s}
= \frac{\sin(\omega t + \omega s)}{s_t s_s}
= \frac{s_{t+s}}{s_t s_s}.
\]

Key identity used: \(\cos a\sin b + \sin a\cos b = \sin(a+b)\).

### 2d. Linear coefficient

\[
B \equiv \frac{x}{s_t}+\frac{z}{s_s}.
\]

### 2e. Completing the square in y

\[
E(y) = i\alpha A\left(y - \frac{B}{A}\right)^2 - \frac{i\alpha B^2}{A} + i\alpha\left(\frac{x^2 c_t}{s_t}+\frac{z^2 c_s}{s_s}\right).
\]

Stationary point: \(y_0 = B/A = (x\,s_s + z\,s_t)/s_{t+s}\).

## 3. Gaussian integral in y

Set \(u = y - y_0\). The \(u\)-integral:
\[
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\exp(i\alpha A\,u^2)\,du
= \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{-i\alpha A}}
= \sqrt{\frac{\pi\,s_t\,s_s}{-i\alpha\,s_{t+s}}}.
\]

(Using \(\int e^{i\beta u^2}du = \sqrt{\pi/(- i\beta)}\) for \(\beta > 0\), with appropriate branch.)

## 4. Combined prefactor (normalization closure)

\[
\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\sqrt{s_t s_s}}\cdot\sqrt{\frac{\pi\,s_t\,s_s}{-i\alpha\,s_{t+s}}}
= \frac{\alpha}{\pi i\sqrt{s_t s_s}}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{\pi}\,\sqrt{s_t s_s}}{\sqrt{-i\alpha\,s_{t+s}}}
= \frac{\sqrt{\alpha}}{\sqrt{\pi}\,i\,\sqrt{-i\,s_{t+s}}}.
\]

Since \(i\sqrt{-i} = i\cdot e^{-i\pi/4} = e^{i\pi/2}\cdot e^{-i\pi/4} = e^{i\pi/4} = \sqrt{i}\):
\[
= \frac{\sqrt{\alpha}}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\sqrt{i}\,\sqrt{s_{t+s}}}
= \sqrt{\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\,s_{t+s}}}.
\]

**This is exactly the prefactor of \(K_{t+s}(x,z)\).**

### 4a. d-dimensional generalization

For \(d\) dimensions, each direction contributes independently:
\[
\text{Prefactor}_{(d)} = \left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\,s_t}\right)^{d/2}\left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\,s_s}\right)^{d/2} \cdot \left(\frac{\pi s_t s_s}{-i\alpha\,s_{t+s}}\right)^{d/2}.
\]

By the same algebra:
\[
= \left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi i\,s_{t+s}}\right)^{d/2}.
\]

**The \(d/2\) exponent is forced:** the Gaussian integral produces exactly one factor \(\sqrt{\pi/(- i\alpha A)}\) per spatial dimension, converting \(t^{-d/2}\cdot s^{-d/2}\) into \((t+s)^{-d/2}\) (with \(t \to s_t\), etc.). Any exponent other than \(d/2\) would fail the functional equation.

## 5. Remainder (phase closure)

The remainder after completing the square:
\[
R = -\frac{i\alpha B^2}{A} + i\alpha\left(\frac{x^2 c_t}{s_t}+\frac{z^2 c_s}{s_s}\right).
\]

### 5a. Expand \(B^2/A\)

\[
\frac{B^2}{A} = \frac{(x/s_t + z/s_s)^2}{s_{t+s}/(s_t s_s)}
= \frac{s_t s_s}{s_{t+s}}\left(\frac{x}{s_t}+\frac{z}{s_s}\right)^2
= \frac{(x\,s_s + z\,s_t)^2}{s_t\,s_s\,s_{t+s}}.
\]

Expand numerator:
\[
(x\,s_s + z\,s_t)^2 = x^2 s_s^2 + 2xz\,s_t s_s + z^2 s_t^2.
\]

### 5b. Full remainder

\[
R = i\alpha\left[\frac{x^2 c_t}{s_t}+\frac{z^2 c_s}{s_s}
- \frac{x^2 s_s^2 + 2xz\,s_t s_s + z^2 s_t^2}{s_t\,s_s\,s_{t+s}}\right].
\]

Collecting \(x^2\) terms:
\[
\frac{x^2 c_t}{s_t} - \frac{x^2 s_s^2}{s_t s_s s_{t+s}}
= \frac{x^2}{s_t}\left(c_t - \frac{s_s}{s_{t+s}}\right)
= \frac{x^2}{s_t}\cdot\frac{c_t s_{t+s} - s_s}{s_{t+s}}.
\]

Using \(c_t s_{t+s} = c_t(s_t c_s + c_t s_s) = s_t c_t c_s + c_t^2 s_s\):
\[
c_t s_{t+s} - s_s = s_t c_t c_s + c_t^2 s_s - s_s = s_t c_t c_s - s_s(1-c_t^2) = s_t c_t c_s - s_s s_t^2.
\]

Factor \(s_t\): \(= s_t(c_t c_s - s_t s_s) = s_t\,c_{t+s}\).

Therefore:
\[
x^2\text{-coeff} = \frac{x^2\,c_{t+s}}{s_{t+s}}.
\]

By symmetry (\(x\leftrightarrow z\), \(t\leftrightarrow s\)):
\[
z^2\text{-coeff} = \frac{z^2\,c_{t+s}}{s_{t+s}}.
\]

Cross term:
\[
-\frac{2xz\,s_t s_s}{s_t s_s s_{t+s}} = -\frac{2xz}{s_{t+s}}.
\]

### 5c. Final phase

\[
R = \frac{i\alpha}{s_{t+s}}\bigl[(x^2+z^2)\,c_{t+s} - 2xz\bigr]
= \frac{im\omega}{2\hbar\sin\omega(t+s)}\bigl[(x^2+z^2)\cos\omega(t+s)-2xz\bigr].
\]

**This is exactly \(iS_{t+s}(x,z)/\hbar\).** Phase closure verified.

## 6. Full composition result

\[
\boxed{
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}K_t(x,y)\,K_s(y,z)\,dy
= \sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{2\pi i\hbar\sin\omega(t+s)}}\;
\exp\!\left[\frac{im\omega}{2\hbar\sin\omega(t+s)}
\bigl((x^2+z^2)\cos\omega(t+s)-2xz\bigr)\right]
= K_{t+s}(x,z).
}
\]

## 7. d/2 forcing mechanism (interacting case)

### 7a. Functional equation for normalization

Write \(\mathcal{N}_d(t) = (\alpha/(\pi i s_t))^{d/2}\) and Gaussian factor \(G_d(t,s) = (\pi s_t s_s/(-i\alpha s_{t+s}))^{d/2}\).

Composition requires:
\[
\mathcal{N}_d(t)\,\mathcal{N}_d(s)\,G_d(t,s) = \mathcal{N}_d(t+s).
\]

Suppose normalization has exponent \(\nu\) (not necessarily \(d/2\)):
\[
\left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi i s_t}\right)^{\nu}\left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi i s_s}\right)^{\nu}\left(\frac{\pi s_t s_s}{-i\alpha s_{t+s}}\right)^{d/2} = \left(\frac{\alpha}{\pi i s_{t+s}}\right)^{\nu}.
\]

LHS \(\alpha\)-power: \(2\nu - d/2\). RHS: \(\nu\). Therefore \(2\nu - d/2 = \nu\), giving \(\nu = d/2\).

LHS \(s_t\)-power: \(-\nu + d/2\). For this to cancel (no residual \(s_t\) dependence on the combined side): \(-\nu + d/2 = 0\), again \(\nu = d/2\).

**The \(d/2\) exponent is the unique solution.** The Gaussian integral from \(d\) spatial dimensions produces a fixed \(d/2\) power of the quadratic coefficient; the normalization exponent must match. This is identical in structure to the free case but with \(t \to \sin\omega t\).

### 7b. Comparison to free particle

| Quantity | Free | Mehler |
|---|---|---|
| Time function | \(t\) | \(\sin\omega t\) |
| Prefactor | \((\alpha/\pi i t)^{d/2}\) | \((\alpha/\pi i s_t)^{d/2}\) |
| Gaussian coeff | \((t+s)/(ts)\) | \(s_{t+s}/(s_t s_s)\) |
| d/2 forced? | YES | YES (same mechanism) |
| Phase | \(im(x-z)^2/(2\hbar(t+s))\) | \(i\alpha[(x^2+z^2)c_{t+s}-2xz]/s_{t+s}\) |

**Structure is identical.** The replacement \(t \to \sin\omega t\) does not alter the \(d/2\) forcing mechanism because the Gaussian integral dimension-counting is purely algebraic.

## 8. κ-necessity (interacting case)

### 8a. Dimensional analysis

In the Mehler kernel, \(\kappa = \hbar\) appears in:
1. Prefactor: \((m\omega/(2\pi i\hbar\sin\omega t))^{d/2}\)
2. Phase: \(\exp(iS_{\mathrm{cl}}/\hbar)\)

The \(\omega\)-dependent trigonometric functions are **dimensionless** (they carry no action dimension). Thus \(\kappa = \hbar\) enters the composition in exactly the same way as for the free particle:
- Gaussian integral width \(\propto \hbar/(m\omega/\sin\omega t) = \hbar\sin\omega t/(m\omega)\)
- Phase denominator: \(1/\hbar\)

### 8b. κ → 0 obstruction

As \(\hbar \to 0\): prefactor \(\to \infty\) (diverges as \(\hbar^{-d/2}\)), the kernel becomes a delta-function supported on classical trajectories. But the composition integral becomes ill-defined (product of distributions). Same obstruction as free case.

### 8c. κ → ∞ obstruction

As \(\hbar \to \infty\): phase \(\to 0\), kernel becomes a constant (no dynamics). Composition gives \(\int dy = \infty\). Same obstruction as free case.

**κ-necessity persists for V ≠ 0.** The potential modifies the action but not the κ-dependence structure.

## 9. Short-time expansion and a₁ coefficient

Expand \(\omega/\sin\omega t = t^{-1}(1+(\omega t)^2/6+\cdots)\), \(\cos\omega t = 1 - (\omega t)^2/2+\cdots\).

Prefactor: \((m\omega/(2\pi i\hbar\sin\omega t))^{1/2} = (m/(2\pi i\hbar t))^{1/2}(1+(\omega t)^2/12+\cdots)\).

Action at \(y \approx x\): \(S_t(x,y) = m(x-y)^2/(2t) - (m\omega^2 x^2/2)\,t + O(\omega^4 t^3)\).

Combined:
\[
K_t(x,y) \approx K_t^{\mathrm{free}}(x,y)\left(1 - \frac{im\omega^2 x^2}{2\hbar}t + O(t^2)\right).
\]

First Seeley-DeWitt coefficient:
\[
\boxed{a_1(x) = -\frac{iV(x)}{\hbar}, \quad V(x) = \tfrac{1}{2}m\omega^2 x^2.}
\]

Matches standard result. The \(a_n\) are compatibility conditions derivable from composition.

## 10. Verdict

**Success criterion 1: Full persistence.**

1. **d/2 forced:** The composition functional equation for the Mehler kernel forces the normalization exponent to be \(d/2\), by the same algebraic mechanism as the free particle (Section 7a). The replacement \(t \to \sin\omega t\) does not affect the dimension-counting.

2. **κ forced:** The action-dimensional constant \(\kappa = \hbar\) enters the Mehler composition identically to the free case: through the phase \(\exp(iS/\hbar)\) and the prefactor \(\hbar^{-d/2}\). The ω-dependent trigonometric functions are dimensionless and carry no κ-dependence. Both κ → 0 and κ → ∞ obstructions persist (Section 8).

3. **Seeley-DeWitt compatibility:** The first coefficient \(a_1 = -iV/\hbar\) is correctly reproduced from the short-time expansion of the exact Mehler kernel (Section 9c). This shows the a_n are compatibility conditions derivable from composition, not independent degrees of freedom.

**P4.2 extends to the interacting harmonic oscillator without modification.** The Mehler kernel provides an exact, all-order witness.
