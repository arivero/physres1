# Blackboard 4 — Two-particle composition test for κ universality
Date: 2026-02-14
Cycle: S286 (from D32)

## Setup
Two free particles on \(\mathbb R\), masses \(m_1,m_2\), independent action scales \(\kappa_1,\kappa_2\).

Single-particle kernels (d=1, from P4.2):
\[
K_i(q_f,q_i;\Delta t)=\left(\frac{m_i}{2\pi\kappa_i\Delta t}\right)^{1/2}\exp\!\left(\frac{im_i(q_f-q_i)^2}{2\kappa_i\Delta t}\right).
\]

## Test 1: Decoupled (tensor product)

Composite kernel:
\[
K_{12}=K_1\otimes K_2.
\]

Semigroup check:
\[
\int dq_1\,dq_2\;K_{12}(\mathbf q_f,\mathbf q;t_2)\,K_{12}(\mathbf q,\mathbf q_i;t_1)
=\prod_{i=1}^{2}\int dq_i\;K_i(q_{if},q_i;t_2)\,K_i(q_i,q_{ii};t_1).
\]

Each factor closes independently via standard Fresnel convolution:
\[
\int dq\;\left(\frac{m}{2\pi\kappa t_2}\right)^{1/2}e^{im(q_f-q)^2/2\kappa t_2}\left(\frac{m}{2\pi\kappa t_1}\right)^{1/2}e^{im(q-q_i)^2/2\kappa t_1}
=\left(\frac{m}{2\pi\kappa(t_1+t_2)}\right)^{1/2}e^{im(q_f-q_i)^2/2\kappa(t_1+t_2)}.
\]

Key: the convolution for particle \(i\) involves only \((m_i,\kappa_i)\). No cross-talk.

**Result 1.** Decoupled composition closes for arbitrary \(\kappa_1\neq\kappa_2\). Universality NOT forced.

## Test 2: Coupled system (interaction potential)

Total Lagrangian: \(\mathcal L=\tfrac{1}{2}m_1\dot q_1^2+\tfrac{1}{2}m_2\dot q_2^2-V(q_1,q_2)\).

### P4.2 assumption (L) applied to composite system
P4.2 requires:
\[
K_{\Delta t}(\mathbf q_f,\mathbf q_i)=\mathcal N(\Delta t)\,\exp\!\bigl(c_0\,S_{\mathrm{slice}}(\mathbf q_f,\mathbf q_i;\Delta t)\bigr),
\]
with a **single** constant \(c_0=i/\kappa\) and total sliced action
\[
S_{\mathrm{slice}}=\frac{m_1(\Delta q_1)^2}{2\Delta t}+\frac{m_2(\Delta q_2)^2}{2\Delta t}-V\bigl(\tfrac{q_{1f}+q_{1i}}{2},\tfrac{q_{2f}+q_{2i}}{2}\bigr)\Delta t.
\]

### Obstruction with \(\kappa_1\neq\kappa_2\)
If each kinetic sector retains its own scale, the exponential weight would be:
\[
\exp\!\left(\frac{im_1(\Delta q_1)^2}{2\kappa_1\Delta t}+\frac{im_2(\Delta q_2)^2}{2\kappa_2\Delta t}-\frac{i}{\kappa_?}V\,\Delta t\right).
\]

This cannot be written as \(\exp(iS_{\mathrm{total}}/\kappa)\) unless \(\kappa_1=\kappa_2=\kappa_?\).

### Formal obstruction (composition)
With coupling, the short-time kernel is **not** a tensor product. The composition integral
\[
\int d\mathbf q\;K_{12}(\mathbf q_f,\mathbf q;t_2)\,K_{12}(\mathbf q,\mathbf q_i;t_1)
\]
involves cross-terms from \(V\). The Gaussian prefactor scales as
\[
\mathcal N(\Delta t)=\left(\frac{m_1}{2\pi\kappa_1\Delta t}\right)^{1/2}\left(\frac{m_2}{2\pi\kappa_2\Delta t}\right)^{1/2},
\]
but the semigroup functional equation for the normalization (D4.1a) applied to the composite system on \(\mathbb R^2\) forces
\[
\mathcal N(t)\propto t^{-d/2}\quad(d=2),
\]
with a **single** overall \(\kappa\):
\[
\mathcal N(\Delta t)=\left(\frac{M_{\mathrm{eff}}}{2\pi\kappa\,\Delta t}\right)^{d/2}.
\]
The identity limit \(K\to\delta^{(2)}(\mathbf q_f-\mathbf q_i)\) then fixes \(c_0=i/\kappa\) uniformly across all terms in \(S_{\mathrm{slice}}\).

**Result 2.** For coupled systems, P4.2 assumption (L) — single exponential weight \(e^{c_0 S}\) — forces \(\kappa_1=\kappa_2\).

## Mechanism
- Assumption (L) is the key: the kernel is \(\exp(c_0 S_{\mathrm{total}})\) with one \(c_0\).
- For decoupled systems, \(S_{\mathrm{total}}=S_1+S_2\) and the exponential factorizes; each factor can carry its own \(c_0^{(i)}\).
- Coupling (\(V\neq0\)) prevents factorization; the single \(c_0\) then forces a universal \(\kappa\).
- Physically: any interaction creates a single quantum system with one action scale.

## Verdict
**Composition forces \(\kappa_1=\kappa_2\) for coupled systems; decoupled sectors can have independent \(\kappa\).**

Universality of \(\kappa\) follows from P4.2 + coupling. Any two subsystems that interact must share the same \(\kappa\). Transitivity of interaction then makes \(\kappa\) universal across all interacting sectors.

## Scope
- Free/decoupled: independent \(\kappa_i\) allowed.
- Any interaction \(V(q_1,q_2)\neq0\): forces \(\kappa_1=\kappa_2\).
- Transitivity: if 1 couples to 2 and 2 couples to 3, all share one \(\kappa\).
- Physical consequence: in a universe where everything interacts (at least gravitationally), \(\kappa=\hbar\) is universal.
