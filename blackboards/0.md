# TG-Q1: Half-Density Groupoid Measure — Mathematician's Analysis

## 1. Setup and Question

Standard Lackman construction:
- Pair groupoid G = M × M, M = ℝ^d
- Composition: (x,z)·(z,y) = (x,y)
- Convolution: (f*g)(x,y;t) = ∫ f(x,z;t₁) g(z,y;t₂) d^d z
- Measure: full Lebesgue d^d z [weight 1]
- d/2 arises from: Gaussian Jacobian over (N-1) intermediate points

**TG-Q1 (open):** Replace d^d z with a density of weight 1/2: d^d z → |det g_{zz}|^{1/4} d^d z.
Does composition close? Does d/2 still arise? What is the geometric content?

## 2. Half-Density Measure on Groupoid Arrows

Recall: a **density of weight λ** on M is a section of |Λ^d T*M|^λ.
- λ=1: Lebesgue measure d^d z (full density)
- λ=1/2: half-density |d^d z|^{1/2} (transforms as |det ∂z'/∂z|^{1/2} under diffeomorphisms)

For G = M × M (arrows), a **bi-half-density** is:
  |dx|^{1/2} ⊗ |dy|^{1/2} (on source × target)

**Proposal:** Define half-density convolution:
  (f ⊛ g)(x,y;t) = ∫ f(x,z;t₁) · g(z,y;t₂) · |d^d z|^{1/2}

**Immediate problem:** The integrand f·g·|dz|^{1/2} has dimension issues.

## 3. Dimensional Analysis of Half-Density Convolution

If f: G → ℂ is a **bi-half-density kernel** (density^{1/2} in each variable):
  [f] = L^{-d/2} · L^{-d/2} = L^{-d} [in d-dimensional units]

Wait — a bi-half-density f(x,y) transforms as:
  f → |det ∂x'/∂x|^{1/2} · |det ∂y'/∂y|^{1/2} · f (covariant)

Pairing a bi-half-density with a half-density dz^{1/2}:
  ∫ f(x,z) · g(z,y) · |dz|^{1/2} dz

The z-index: f contributes |dz|^{1/2} (from the target-side half-density); g contributes |dz|^{1/2} (from the source-side half-density). Together:
  f(x,z) · g(z,y) pairs as: [|dx|^{1/2} |dz|^{1/2}] · [|dz|^{1/2} |dy|^{1/2}]

The two |dz|^{1/2} factors combine: |dz|^{1/2} · |dz|^{1/2} = |dz| = d^d z (FULL Lebesgue).

**Conclusion:** The natural convolution of two bi-half-density kernels is:
  (f ⊛ g)(x,y) = ∫ f(x,z) g(z,y) d^d z   [SAME as standard convolution]

The half-density structure is ABSORBED by the pairing — the z-integral automatically becomes full Lebesgue. The output (f⊛g)(x,y) is again a bi-half-density.

## 4. Normalization in the Half-Density Framework

For the free particle with half-density kernel K^{hd}(x,y;t) = bi-half-density:
  K^{hd}(x,y;t) = N_hd(t) · exp(im|x-y|²/2ℏt) · |dx|^{1/2} |dy|^{1/2}

Composition: ∫ K^{hd}(x,z;t₁) K^{hd}(z,y;t₂) d^d z = K^{hd}(x,y;t₁+t₂)

Gaussian integral (same as standard):
  N_hd(t₁) · N_hd(t₂) · (πℏ·2t₁t₂/im·t)^{d/2} = N_hd(t)  [where t=t₁+t₂]

Same semigroup equation for N_hd as for N_standard → N_hd(t) ∝ t^{-d/2}.

**KEY FINDING:** The d/2 exponent is UNCHANGED. The half-density structure changes the geometric interpretation but not the normalization power.

## 5. What Changes vs Standard Framework

**The van Vleck factor:** For K(x,y;t) in curved space, the semiclassical expansion gives:
  K(x,y;t) ~ (2πiℏt)^{-d/2} · D(x,y;t)^{1/2} · exp(iS_cl/ℏ)
where D(x,y;t) = |det(-∂²S_cl/∂x∂y)| is the Van Vleck determinant.

In the bi-half-density framework: D(x,y;t)^{1/2} |dx|^{1/2}|dy|^{1/2} is the NATURAL object.
Van Vleck density = |d²S_cl/∂x∂y|^{1/2} = half-density of the generating function S_cl.

**The half-density framework EXPLAINS why the Van Vleck determinant appears with power 1/2:**
it is the determinant of the natural bi-half-density structure on the pair groupoid.

## 6. Proposition: Half-Density Variant Exists, Same Normalization

**Proposition TG-P2.1 (Half-density pair-groupoid path integral).**

Let G = M × M be the pair groupoid on M = ℝ^d. Define a **half-density kernel**:
  K^{hd}: G × (0,∞) → ℂ ⊗ Dens^{1/2}(M) ⊗ Dens^{1/2}(M)

i.e., K^{hd}(x,y;t) = K̃(x,y;t) |dx|^{1/2} |dy|^{1/2} where K̃ ∈ C∞(G×(0,∞)).

(a) **Convolution closure:** K^{hd} ⊛ K^{hd} = K^{hd} where:
  (K^{hd} ⊛ K^{hd})(x,y;t) = ∫ K̃(x,z;t₁) K̃(z,y;t₂) d^d z |dx|^{1/2}|dy|^{1/2}
is again a bi-half-density kernel. The intermediate z-integral uses FULL Lebesgue d^d z.

(b) **Same d/2 normalization:** The normalization factor N_hd(t) satisfies the same
functional equation as N_standard(t): N_hd(t₁)·N_hd(t₂)·(Gaussian factor) = N_hd(t),
giving N_hd(t) ∝ t^{-d/2}. The d/2 exponent is unchanged.

(c) **Van Vleck factor:** In curved space (metric g), the semiclassical kernel:
  K^{hd}(x,y;t) ~ t^{-d/2} · D(x,y;t)^{1/2} · e^{iS_cl/ℏ} |dx|^{1/2} |dy|^{1/2}
has D(x,y;t)^{1/2} as the natural bi-half-density of the generating function S_cl —
not an auxiliary factor but intrinsic to the bi-half-density structure.

(d) **Coordinate invariance:** Under diffeomorphism φ: M → M':
  K^{hd}(φ(x),φ(y);t) = K̃(x,y;t) |detDφ(x)|^{1/2}|detDφ(y)|^{1/2} · (new half-dens)
The bi-half-density factor |dx|^{1/2}|dy|^{1/2} absorbs all Jacobians: the scalar K̃
transforms as K̃' = K̃ (coordinate-invariant content).
