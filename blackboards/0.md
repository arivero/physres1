Assessment: Meta-statement unfalsifiable; axioms falsifiable. Paper conflates the two. Sharpen.

**C5. "Channel independence is assumed, not proved."**

RCP claims three INDEPENDENT channels (partition, representation, scale). But:
- Partition → representation: kernel composition forces semigroup, semigroup generator forces Hamiltonian, Hamiltonian ordering IS the representation channel. P4.2 (partition) already constrains ordering.
- Representation → scale: ordering ambiguity at O(hbar^2) in curved space = curvature potential R/6 = Seeley-DeWitt coefficient a_1. But a_1 also appears in RG (heat-kernel regularization of the effective action). So representation data feeds into scale data.
- Scale → partition: RG flow (scale) determines effective couplings; effective couplings determine the kernel (partition). Loop-corrected propagator already mixes channels.

Assessment: Channels are NOT independent at the quantum level. They are independent only classically (partition = action principle, representation = canonical quantization, scale = not yet present). Quantization ENTANGLES them. The three-channel decomposition is a CLASSICAL organizational tool, not a quantum structural theorem.

Rebuttal: Paper says channels are "operational" (§1.3), not "mathematically independent." Each channel has a distinct falsification test. Operational independence (different failure modes) ≠ mathematical independence (no interaction). This is the correct framing.

Assessment: The paper could be sharper. State: "The three channels interact at the quantum level (kernel ordering affects RG, etc.) but have distinct classical origins and distinct failure modes. The decomposition is operational, not algebraic."
