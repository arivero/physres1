**The key object:** half-density kernel K_t(x,y) in D spacetime dimensions.

**Dimensional weight:**
- [K_t(x,y)] = length^{-D/2} × length^{-D/2} × length^0 (time-parameterized)
- From composition: K_{t₁+t₂}(x,z) = ∫ K_{t₁}(x,y) K_{t₂}(y,z) d^Dy
  Dimensions force: 2×(-D/2) + D = 0 ✓ (composition is dimensionally self-consistent)

**Universal constants (c=1 units, retain G and ℏ):**
- ℏ: [ℏ] = M·L²·T⁻¹ = energy × time
- G_D: Newton's constant in D dims, [G_D] = L^{D-2} M⁻¹ T² (from Einstein-Hilbert)
- Planck length in D=4: L_P² = ℏG/c³, [L_P²] = L²

## 2. Sub-question 1a: ℏ = L_P²c³/G as Derived vs Fundamental

**Claim (from motivations.md OQ1a):** If half-density normalization requires an area
scale L_P², then ℏ = L_P²c³/G is derived, not fundamental.

**Dimensional check:**
L_P² c³/G = (L² · L³T⁻³) / (L³M⁻¹T²) = L²L³T⁻³M/L³T⁻² = ML²T⁻¹ = [ℏ] ✓

**Physical rewrite of Feynman amplitude:**
exp(iS/ℏ) = exp(i S·G/(L_P²·c³))

In units where L_P = 1 (Planck area = 1):
= exp(i S·G/c³)

**Question:** Is this rewrite physically illuminating or just a change of units?

**Physicist's assessment:**
This is NOT merely a change of units if we ask: what does it mean to measure action
in units of L_P²c³/G?

The combination L_P²c³/G = ℏ is the minimum quantum of action. But L_P²c³/G can also
be read as: (smallest area) × (surface tension of space) where c³/G = [force/velocity²]
has units of M/T — it is the "mass flow rate" of a gravitational wave.

Actually: c³/G ≈ 4.04×10³⁵ kg/s is the Planck mass flow rate (luminosity per c²).
So ℏ = L_P² × (Planck mass flow rate).

**Interpretation:** The action quantum ℏ is the product of:
1. The minimal geometric area (Planck area L_P²)
2. The gravitational "stiffness" (c³/G = mass flow rate at Planck scale)

This is physically meaningful: it says the quantum of action is set by the scale at
which gravitational degrees of freedom become non-negligible.

## 3. The D=4 Cancellation: Physical Interpretation

**From HD-D1.3:** In conformally flat background g_{μν} = e^{2σ}δ_{μν}:
~Δ ψ = e^{-2σ}[∂²ψ - 2∂σ·∂ψ - (D/2)(∂²σ)ψ + D(4-D)/4 (∂σ)²ψ]

**The D(4-D)/4(∂σ)² term vanishes at D=4.**

**Physical question:** What is the physical significance of this (∂σ)² term?

**(∂σ)² is the kinetic energy of the conformal mode.**

In D=4 the conformal mode σ decouples from the half-density scalar field ψ at quadratic
order (no (∂σ)² coupling). This means:

**At D=4 only:** the half-density scalar ψ does NOT see the kinetic energy of conformal
fluctuations at leading order.

**D≠4 interpretation:** For D<4 or D>4, ψ couples to (∂σ)² — a kinematic coupling to
conformal mode fluctuations. This coupling makes the theory sensitive to the dynamics of
the conformal mode (a new degree of freedom), i.e., the theory is NOT conformally free
at this level.

## 4. Dimensional Analysis: What is Special About D=4

**Dimensional recount:**

- For D=4: coefficient = 4(4-4)/4 = 0 ✓ (exact cancellation)
- For D=6: coefficient = 6(4-6)/4 = -3 (negative — tachyonic?)

**Remark:** D(4-D)/4 = 4-D-D(D-4)/4... actually D(4-D)/4 = D - D²/4
Check: D=4: 4 - 16/4 = 4 - 4 = 0. ✓ Correct.

**Physical dimension sieve:**
- D=4 is the unique dimension where half-density conjugation leaves (∂σ)² coupling = 0
- This is a SEPARATE filter from conformal coupling ξ_conf = (D-2)/(4(D-1)) = 1/6 at D=4

**Are these two D=4 conditions the SAME or DIFFERENT?**

From HD-D1.3b: V(p) = R/6 (dimension-independent from normal coordinates).
And ξ_conf = 1/6 ↔ D=4.

These are TWO coincidences at D=4:
(a) (∂σ)² cancellation in conformal class (specific to conformally flat metrics)
(b) V = (1/6)R matches conformal coupling (holds for ALL metrics, dimension-independent)

Coincidence (b) is stronger (metric-independent). Let me verify they are independent:

The (∂σ)² term appears in conformal class only — it is a conformal-frame artifact.
The R/6 vs ξ_conf=(D-2)/(4(D-1)) comparison is metric-independent.

**Key distinction:** (a) is conformal-class-specific; (b) is universal.
Both single out D=4, but (b) is the robust physical statement.

## 5. HD-D1.3b Physical Rephrasing

**Theorem (HD-D1.3b):** ξ_eff = 1/6 (from half-density conjugation, normal coords, ALL metrics).
ξ_conf(D=4) = (4-2)/(4·3) = 2/12 = 1/6. Exact match at D=4 only.

**Physical translation:**
- ξ_eff = 1/6: the "natural" curvature coupling chosen by coordinate-free half-density formalism
- ξ_conf(D=4) = 1/6: the curvature coupling that makes massive field equations Weyl-invariant at D=4
- Their equality at D=4 means: the half-density field ψ=|g|^{1/4}φ at D=4 is ALREADY
  minimally Weyl-covariant (no separate conformal coupling needed — the half-density weight
  carries it automatically)

**D≠4:** ξ_eff ≠ ξ_conf(D), so the half-density weight is NOT the conformal weight.
A conformally invariant scalar in D≠4 requires an ADDITIONAL coupling beyond half-density.

**This is the physical dimension selection mechanism:**
D=4 is unique in that half-density weight = conformal weight for scalars.
In all other dimensions, the two weights differ and must be separately specified.

## 6. Connection to Seeley-DeWitt

**From HD-D4.2a:** a_1(x,x) = (1/6 - ξ)R.

At D=4, for ψ=|g|^{1/4}φ with ξ=ξ_eff=1/6:
a_1(x,x) = (1/6 - 1/6)R = 0.

**Consequence:** The first Seeley-DeWitt coefficient VANISHES for the half-density scalar at D=4.
This means:
- No linear curvature counterterm needed at one-loop in D=4 for ψ field
- The half-density measure already absorbs the a_1 contribution
- UV finiteness at one-loop in D=4 is "built into" the half-density formalism

**D≠4:** a_1 ≠ 0 → linear-in-R counterterm required → dimensional regularization breaks
the half-density structure (curvature coupling ξ flows under RG).

## 7. Limiting Cases and Physical Checks

**ℏ→0:** Classical limit. The D/2 exponent in K_t ~ (2πiℏt)^{-D/2} · exp(iS_cl/ℏ) is REQUIRED
for composition. Composition still forces D/2 even as ℏ→0 (stationary-phase approximation).
Check: D=4, K_t ~ (2πiℏt)^{-2} → double pole in t (correct for free particle Green function).

**m→∞:** Non-relativistic limit from QFT. Propagator ~ exp(-m|x|)/|x| in D=4 (Yukawa).
The D=4 Yukawa has power |x|^{-(D-2)} = |x|^{-2}. For D=2: log(|x|) — different singularity.
D=4 is the first D where the propagator falls off as a power law faster than log, enabling
a well-defined short-distance limit without IR problems in the static potential.

**G→0:** Turns off gravity. L_P² → 0, so the "Planck area" scale disappears. In this limit,
ℏ must be supplied as an independent input — composition still forces its existence (P4.2)
but does not fix its value without gravitational anchor.

**D→4 (dimensional regularization):** In dim-reg, the propagator is analytic in D near 4.
The pole at D=4 in mass counterterms (Γ(2-D/2)/m^{2(2-D/2)}) shows D=4 is where mass term
becomes LOGARITHMICALLY divergent (log Λ vs power Λ^{D-2}). This is the onset of non-trivial
renormalization — exactly where the theory becomes interesting.
