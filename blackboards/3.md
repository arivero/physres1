## 4. Mathematical Gaps (Core §§1–4)

**G4.1 (Minor, sev-3).** PA-H2.5 admissibility rule (§3.3, PA-H2.7a): "canonical normalization of fields" is stated as a convention to prevent loopholes (e.g., adjoining Ĝ = G^{1/(d-2)}). The rule excludes fractional reparametrizations at weak coupling. Mathematically, the precise statement would be:

> Admissible couplings = generators of the ring ℤ[g₁, g₂, ...] where gᵢ are the literal action coefficients after canonical kinetic-term normalization.

This is not stated formally but is implicit. No error — just imprecision in the admissibility criterion.

**G4.2 (Minor, sev-3).** PA-E2 (gravity + cosmological constant): family C ~ G_d · Λ_d^{d/4−1} works for d divisible by 4. At d=4: n=1, m=0, so no Λ_d needed (as stated). At d=8: n=1, m=1, so G₈·Λ₈^1 has dimension length^{(d/2)=4}. Correct. But the paper says "d divisible by 4 is the only case with m=0" — should say "d=4 is the unique case with m=0 and n=1". Minor wording issue.

**G4.3 (No error found).** PA-D1.4a (free-propagator semigroup): the d/2 exponent cancellation is exact and explicit. THREE-AGENT verified.
